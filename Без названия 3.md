**[Introduction]**

Good afternoon ladies and gentlemen. It's a great pleasure to see you all here today. My name is Ila Perunov. I am a second-year student of group 13 of the computer science department of Voronezh State University. [span_14](start_span)The topic of my presentation is exchange programs for the Autumn of 2026[span_14](end_span). This presentation will be interesting to those of you who want to study abroad and get a unique experience. I have divided my presentation into three main points. First I'm going to tell you about the countries and geography, after that the languages of study, and finally the faculties and available places. My presentation will take about 5 minutes, and after it, there will be time for questions.

  

**[Point 1: Switch to Slide 3]**

So let's get started. In the first part of my presentation, I'm going to tell you about the countries. We have a huge choice of 15 countries. If you want to stay closer to home, you can choose universities in Armenia, Belarus, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, or Uzbekistan. If you like Asia, you can go to China, Vietnam, India, or Indonesia.For fans of warm countries and new cultures, there are programs in Algeria, Tunisia, Turkey, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and even Brazil in South America. That's all I wanted to tell you about the geography.

  

**[Point 2: Switch to Slide 4]**

Now let's move on to the next part of my presentation. I will tell you about the languages of study. Many students are afraid of the language barrier, but you shouldn't be. In many countries, like Belarus or Kazakhstan, you can comfortably study in Russian. English is also very popular and is used in India, Indonesia, Turkey, and Vietnam. But please be careful: in some places, like Brazil or certain universities in Belarus, the number of English courses is very limited. Also, if you know other languages, you can study in French, Arabic, Chinese, or Portuguese.

  

**[Point 3: Switch to Slide 5]**

Now let's move on to the third part of my presentation. I will tell you about the faculties and available places. Almost all programs are open for both Bachelor and Master students. For example, Hong Kong Shue Yan University is the only one that accepts only Bachelor students. Our partner universities offer from 2 to 10 places for our students. The biggest quotas, 10 places, are given by universities in Armenia, Belarus, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and Uzbekistan. Most universities accept students from different faculties, but some are very specific. For example, the university in Uzbekistan only accepts Law students, while Qingdao University in China accepts everyone to study Chinese language and culture. That's all I wanted to tell you about places and faculties.

  

**[Conclusion: Switch to Slide 6]**

In conclusion, I want to say that studying abroad is an amazing chance. First, you need to choose your favorite country and the language you speak. Second, you must check if your faculty is on the list for that university. And third, always check the official websites to see the study courses. That's all I wanted to tell you about my presentation. Now I am ready to answer your questions.

Good afternoon ladies and gentlemen. It's a great pleasure to see all here today. My name is Ila Perunov. I am a second year student of computer science department of Voronezh State University. The topic of my presentation is non-academic activities at VSU. This presentation will be interesting to those of you who would like to know more about student hobbies and health. I have divided my presentation into three main points. First I'm going to tell you about music and media contests, after that exhibitions and singing clubs, and finally sports and psychological support. My presentation will take about 6 minutes and after my presentation there will be time for questions.

  

**[Point 1: Switch to Slide 3]**

So let's get started. In the first part of my presentation I'm going to tell you about music and media contests. VSU hosts unique musical events every year, such as contests of bard's songs and evenings dedicated to Russian romance. This is a great platform for those who love live performance. Also, our students actively participate in regional video clip contests, showing their skills in directing and editing. These activities help develop creativity and find friends. That's all I wanted to tell you about music and media contests.

  

**[Point 2: Switch to Slide 4]**

Now let's move on to the next part of my presentation. In the second part of my presentation I will tell you about exhibitions and singing clubs. If you like to sing, the doors of the "Vdokhnoveniye" singing club are open for you. For visual art lovers, the university organizes photography exhibitions called "The world through the eyes of students", as well as exhibitions of graphical arts and crafts. This is a wonderful way to show your photos or paintings to a large audience. That's all I wanted to tell you about exhibitions and singing clubs.

  

**[Point 3: Switch to Slide 5]**

Now let's move on to the next part of my presentation. In the third part of my presentation I will tell you about sports and psychological support. Our university is represented by 34 teams in 25 different kinds of sport at intercollegiate competitions. We have won city contests for promoting a healthy lifestyle many times. But health is not only about physical shape. Since 2006, the Psychologist Consultation Service has been open in VSU. Psychologists help students solve personal problems and keep a comfortable atmosphere for studying. That's all I wanted to tell you about sports and psychological support.

  

**[Conclusion: Switch to Slide 6]**

In conclusion I want to say that life at VSU is very diverse. First, you can find yourself in music or video making. Second, university exhibitions are a great start for creative people. And third, VSU takes care of the physical and mental health of every student. That's all I wanted to tell you about my presentation. Now I am ready to answer your questions.
